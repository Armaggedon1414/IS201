 Wrestling

 I have been wrestling since I was 5 years old. I love the sport and have competed in many events and tournaments over the years.

  1. History
    • Wrestling is an ancient sport with reliefs and cave-drawings depicting wrestlers in early Egypt and France as old as 15-17,000 years ago
    • Wrestling was added to the Ancient Olympic Games in 8th century BCE

     

  2. Rules
    • Wrestling is a combat sport involving grappling techniques
    • Wrestlers score points by taking down their opponent, escaping from holds, and pinning them to the mat
    • Matches are typically divided into 3, 2 minute periods, with the winner determined by points or pinfall
    • Wrestlers wear singlets, headgear, and wrestling shoes

      •  

    • Points
      1. Takedown: a wrestler scores 2 points taking down an opponent and gaining control. Both the opponents knees and hands must touch the ground
      2. Escape: a wrestler, when already taken down, may gain 1 point by managing to break contact with his opponent and return to his feet
      3. Reversal: a wrestler, when already taken down, may gain 2 points by taking control of his opponent without first breaking contact
      4. Near Fall: 3 point are awarded if the wrestler forces his opponent's back to break 90 degreees with the floor for 3 second; 3 points are awarded for 5 seconds
      5. Penalty: wrestlers may gain points if their opponent uses an illegal hold, a potentially dangerous move, or stalls repeatedly despite warnings from the referee

       

    • Wins
      1. Fall: a wrestler automatically wins the match by pinning both his opponent's shoulder blades to the mat for 3 seconds or as determined by the referee
      2. Technical Fall: a wrestler automatically wins the match by scoring 15 points more than his opponent
      3. Major Decision: a wrestler wins by Major Decision by having 8-14 points more than his opponent at the end of the match
      4. Decision: a wrestler wins by Decision by having 1-7 points more than his opponent at the end of the match
      5. Forfeit: a wrestler automatically wins the match if his opponent does not show up
